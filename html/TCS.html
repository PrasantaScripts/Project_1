TCS : Assistive Technology On Multimodal Disabilities

Funded by : Tcs

Project Overview

Project Description

The project proposes the development of a universal assistive human-machine interface that can create an independent communication platform for human-machine interaction. Human-computer interaction (HCI) is a field that studies how people interact with computers and how computers can be integrated to support human activities. HCI for multimodal disabilities focuses on designing accessible user interfaces. Universal design and inclusive design are important concepts in HCI, aiming to create products and environments that are accessible to everyone. HCI researchers are exploring how mobile technology can support the needs of people with disabilities, such as visual impairments, hearing impairments, physical disabilities, and cognitive disabilities. The goal of HCI is to make technology more usable and improve the user experience by making systems more intuitive and easy to use.
